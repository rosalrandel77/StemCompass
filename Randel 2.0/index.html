What is STEM?

STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. It represents a broad range of fields that use systematic thinking, experimentation, and problem-solving skills. The purpose of STEM is to drive innovation, solve real-world challenges, and prepare students for careers that shape our future.

Whether you're just curious or considering a path in one of these disciplines, the world of STEM offers endless possibilities. From building sustainable cities to creating new medical technologies, STEM is at the heart of modern advancement.

Where STEM Can Take You in College

Choosing a STEM track in school opens doors to numerous college majors and career pathways. Here are a few examples:

  • Biology & Biomedical Engineering
  • Computer Science & Information Technology
  • Civil, Mechanical, Electrical Engineering
  • Environmental Science & Sustainability
  • Mathematics, Data Science & Analytics

Colleges often offer hands-on labs, research opportunities, and internships for STEM students. Your foundational knowledge will help you specialize in fields like robotics, artificial intelligence, or renewable energy.
